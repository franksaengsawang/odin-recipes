Tiramisu

Overview

A no-bake Italian dessert that’s rich, airy, and gently bittersweet.

  • Prep Time 25–30 min
  • Chill Time 4–6 hrs
  • Servings 8–10

Ingredients

Cream

  • 500g mascarpone, chilled
  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 100g (½ cup) sugar, divided
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
  • Pinch of salt

Assembly

  • 250–300g ladyfingers (savoiardi)
  • 1½ cups strong espresso or coffee, cooled
  • 2–3 tbsp Marsala or dark rum (optional)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Steps

  1. Whip yolks: Beat egg yolks with half the sugar until pale and thick. Fold in mascarpone and vanilla until smooth.
  2. Whip whites: With clean beaters, whip egg whites with a pinch of salt to soft peaks. Stream in remaining sugar; beat to glossy medium peaks. Gently fold into mascarpone mixture.
  3. Coffee bath: Combine coffee with Marsala/rum (if using) in a shallow dish. Briefly dip ladyfingers (1–2 sec per side).
  4. Layer: Arrange a layer of soaked ladyfingers in a 9×13 in dish. Spread half the cream. Repeat with another ladyfinger layer and remaining cream.
  5. Chill & finish: Cover and refrigerate 4–6 hours (or overnight). Dust generously with cocoa before serving; add chocolate shavings if desired.

Notes

  • Use very cold mascarpone for a stable cream.
  • Quick dips prevent soggy ladyfingers—keep the center slightly firm.
  • For a cooked-egg version, make a zabaglione by whisking yolks and sugar over a bain-marie until 70–75°C.
