Spaghetti Carbonara

Overview

Silky egg-cheese emulsion coats al dente spaghetti with crispy guanciale and black pepper.

  • Prep Time 10–15 min
  • Cook Time 15 min
  • Servings 2–3

Ingredients

  • 200–250g spaghetti
  • 100g guanciale (or pancetta), cut into lardons
  • 2 large eggs + 1 large yolk
  • ¾ cup (65–70g) finely grated Pecorino Romano (or mix with Parmigiano)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Steps

  1. Prep sauce: In a bowl, whisk eggs, yolk, cheese, and a generous amount of pepper until creamy.
  2. Crisp guanciale: Cook guanciale over medium heat until golden and fat renders. Remove from heat but keep the fat in the pan.
  3. Cook pasta: Boil spaghetti in salted water until just shy of al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water.
  4. Emulsify: Add drained pasta to the pan with guanciale and fat. Toss with a splash of pasta water to coat.
  5. Make it silky: Off heat, pour in egg-cheese mixture, tossing vigorously. Add hot pasta water little by little to create a glossy sauce without scrambling.
  6. Finish: Adjust pepper and cheese. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Work off heat when adding the eggs to avoid scrambling.
  • Use guanciale for authenticity; pancetta or thick bacon in a pinch.
  • Pecorino is traditional and saltier—taste before adding extra salt.
