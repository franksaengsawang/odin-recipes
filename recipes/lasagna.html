Lasagne al Forno

Overview

A classic baked Italian pasta layered with rich ragù, silky béchamel, and Parmigiano.

  • Prep Time 25–30 min
  • Cook Time 45–60 min
  • Servings 6–8

Ingredients

Ragù

  • 500g ground beef (or beef/pork mix)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups (480ml) tomato passata or marinara
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Béchamel

  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups (720ml) milk, warmed
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Salt, to taste

Assembly

  • 12 lasagna sheets (fresh or dried)
  • 1½–2 cups shredded mozzarella
  • ¾ cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • Olive oil, for the dish

Steps

  1. Make the ragù: Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté onion until soft, add garlic for 30 seconds, then brown the meat. Stir in tomato paste and passata. Season and simmer 15–20 minutes.
  2. Make the béchamel: Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook 1–2 minutes. Gradually whisk in warm milk until smooth and thickened. Season with salt and a pinch of nutmeg.
  3. Preheat: Oven to 190°C (375°F). Lightly oil a 9×13 in (or similar) baking dish.
  4. Par-cook pasta (if dried): Boil until just flexible (a few minutes less than package), then drain.
  5. Assemble layers: Spread a thin layer of ragù in the dish, then pasta, ragù, béchamel, mozzarella, and Parmigiano. Repeat 3–4 layers, finishing with béchamel and Parmigiano on top.
  6. Bake: 40–45 minutes until bubbling and golden. If top browns too fast, tent with foil.
  7. Rest & serve: Let sit 10–15 minutes before slicing. Serve warm.

Notes

  • For deeper flavor, simmer ragù longer (up to 45–60 minutes).
  • Swap in spinach or mushrooms for a veggie layer.
  • Fresh pasta sheets can skip par-cooking—just add a touch more béchamel.
